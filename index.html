Chocolate Chipper

Using an electric mixer, beat together 1 cup of shortening, 1 cup of granulated sugar, half a cup of brown sugar, 2 eggs, and 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.

In a separate bowl, sift together 2 cups of flour, 1 and a half teaspoons of salt, and 1 teaspoon of baking soda. Gradually add this dry mixture to the creamed ingredients, blending until it is well combined. Optionally, you can also add in nuts.

  • 1 cup shortening
  • 1 cup granuates sugar
  • 0.5 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla
  • 2 cup flour
  • 1.5 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 package, 12 oz chocolate chip
  • 1 cup nuts (optional)

cheese Danish.

Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit and prepare 2 baking sheets by lining them with parchment paper.

In a small bowl, mix one whole egg with 1 teaspoon of water, then set aside.

In a separate medium bowl, use an electric mixer on medium-high speed to beat together the egg yolk, cream cheese, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla until the mixture is smooth.

Take the 2 sheets of puff pastry and cut each into 4 squares. Fold the corners of each square over by about 1 inch to create octagon shapes.

Evenly spoon the cream cheese mixture into the center of each pastry square, using about 2 tablespoons per square. Avoid spreading the cream cheese. Brush all exposed pastry with the egg wash, then generously dust with sugar.

Bake the pastries until they are puffed and golden brown, which should take about 18 minutes. Allow them to cool slightly before serving.
